A Little More on Me - the Personal Side

My name is Brian Gardner and I created this site. If you spend enough time around here, you’ll notice that I have a sincere passion for life. I’ve been happily married to Shelly for over a decade, and have a son named Zach.

Among the things that I really enjoy doing are traveling and listening to music such as Tori Amos, The Sundays and The Dave Matthews Band. I’ve always been a contemplative person, and believe in Carpe Diem — which ultimately means that I belong in the Dead Poet’s Society.

7 years ago I had the urge to start a blog. At the time, I wasn’t happy with the way themes were designed, so I took matters into my own hands. It started out as a freelance business and became a company called StudioPress.

We developed the Genesis Framework, which Mashable calls the “best of the best” among WordPress frameworks. Because WordPress and Genesis are 100% GPL-licensed, the development communities in both are flourishing.

Technical Details About This Site

As a technologist I love learning and using technology and I am a generally curious person so I am interested in what technologies others use and thought I might share what I am using. We are all standing on the back of giants and I find it very exciting

While this site is very simple in some ways in others it serves as my classroom and testbed. It is basically a static site built using Jekyll and hosted on Amazon S3/CloudFront.

Jekyll is a simple, blog-aware, static site generator. It takes a template directory containing raw text files in various formats, runs it through Markdown and Liquid converters, and spits out a complete, ready-to-publish static website. This simplicity keeps it very reliable (few points of failure) and also very secure. This is a great lesson for corporate sites - the simpler the architecture the easier it is to secure and operate.

It starts to become more complex as we did a bit deeper. Jekyll is built on Ruby and uses the Ruby packaging system called RubyGems. Jekyll aslo is built on a number of components such as pygments for syntax highlighting,
